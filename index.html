The House

Kaiyo Meer takes its name from two languages and one word — sea — spoken an ocean apart. We design for the space between: the hour when the desert cools and the coastline warms, when a linen coat means as much on a dhow as it does on a dune. Every piece begins as undyed cloth, then takes its colour from the land it was cut to cross.

The Colour Line

Dyed at the waterline.

Every dye lot starts at an oasis — the only place in the desert where sand and water sit close enough to compare notes. We match our undyed cloth against the colour of the pool at first light, then again at dusk, and split the difference. No two seasons come out quite the same.
